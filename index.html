About me

Welcome to my page!
I'm Albert, a Dunlap Postdoctoral Fellow
at the Dunlap Institute for Astronomy and Astrophysics, University of Toronto.

I received my Ph.D. in Physics in 2023
from the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology,
under the supervision of Prof. George F. Smoot

My interest in astronomy began in childhood.
I've always been fascinated by the night sky and the universe's workings.
This passion led me to pursue a career in astronomical research.

Curriculum Vitae

Researches

Research Interest:

Instrumental and Experimental Astronomy, especially on Radio Frequency electronics, Photon Detecting Instruments and mixed signal (analog and digitial) Readout System Development.

Current projects:
The Canadian Hydrogen Observatory and Radio-transient Detector (CHORD) RF design,
Development and calibration of a multiple single photon detectors,
and Radio Frequency over Fiber (RFoF) for Radio Astronomy technology development

Research Highlights

Start-up: Centauri Optics

In year 2018 (just before I started my Ph.D.), I found a start-up company with
Prof. KS Wong (my undergraduate thesis supervisor), Dr. Chan and Anthony Lam.

We aim to develop low-cost, portable and remote controllable microscopes
for laboratory and educational usage.
